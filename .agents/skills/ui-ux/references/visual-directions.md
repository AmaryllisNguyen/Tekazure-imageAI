# Visual Directions

Pick one direction per screen family. Do not mix directions randomly.

## 1) Editorial Minimal
- Traits: strong typography, restrained color, generous whitespace.
- Best for: premium brand pages, product marketing, trust-first experiences.
- Avoid when: dense operational UI is required.

## 2) Data Utility
- Traits: crisp grids, high legibility, compact but calm spacing.
- Best for: dashboards, monitoring, internal tools.
- Avoid when: emotional storytelling is primary.

## 3) Creator Canvas
- Traits: dark or neutral stage, focus on content preview, floating tools.
- Best for: image/video creation tools and editors.
- Avoid when: users need simple one-step tasks only.

## 4) Playful Productive
- Traits: vibrant accents, rounded geometry, clear feedback states.
- Best for: consumer onboarding and low-friction task apps.
- Avoid when: strict enterprise visual language is mandatory.

## 5) Industrial Clean
- Traits: strong contrast, square edges, explicit status indicators.
- Best for: operational workflows and B2B admin panels.
- Avoid when: brand asks for soft/premium emotional tone.

## 6) Soft Premium
- Traits: muted palettes, layered surfaces, subtle motion.
- Best for: high-end SaaS and portfolio products.
- Avoid when: many dense controls must fit on one screen.

## 7) Bold Campaign
- Traits: dramatic type, large imagery, high-impact CTA sections.
- Best for: launch pages and conversion-focused campaigns.
- Avoid when: repetitive daily-use interfaces are primary.

## 8) Technical Studio
- Traits: modular panels, tokenized spacing, explicit tool states.
- Best for: AI tools, design systems, dev-facing products.
- Avoid when: content-first narrative pages dominate.

## Selection Rule

Choose direction by product goal first, then user context, then brand constraints.
If uncertain, default to Technical Studio for AI tooling and Data Utility for admin dashboards.
